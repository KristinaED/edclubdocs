Issuing certificates of completion

Certificates are a great motivational tool that keeps students engaged throughout the typing course. Instructors can award certificates to students who either finish a test or meet a passing requirement for a test.



  • Certificate for finishing a test

    Everyone who completes the test will both pass and be issued a certificate. This is useful if you are doing a placement test that doesn't have passing requirements.

    An easy way to check if your certificates are turned on is to see if you have a tab titled
    Print Certificates on the test management page:




  • Certificate for meeting the passing criteria

    Students must meet the passing requirements in order to be issued a certificate. The passing requirements can be customized.



As of November 2016, certificates are issued by default. If you created a test prior to November 2016, your certificates might not be turned on. You can edit your preferences by going to your test and clicking the Edit Options tab on the top. Among some other customizable settings, this page allows you to see if the Certificates of Achievement option is checked.

Warning: Issuing certificates retroactively is not possible. Always remember to check the settings before your students take a test. Whenever you make changes, do not forget to click Update Options on the bottom of the page.
